Canal Belt

Placed on the Unesco World Heritage Sites list, the famous 'belt of canals' is an excellent introduction to the city's many sights.

Red Ligh Disctrict

It is very likely that you have heard about this neighbourhood. Amsterdam's Red Light District leaves nothing to imagination.

Jordaan Area

A pleasant district with a mixture of all kinds of styles and activities. Founded in 1612 for the workforce the Jordaan is one of the most wanted locations in Holland.

Vondelpark

Situated in the centre of Amsterdam the park has been awarded the status of governmental monument, which reflects its historic and cultural value.

Flower Market

Standing on houseboats, the only floating flower market in the world offers all sorts of tulips, narcissus, other bulbs and flowers.

Museums

Amsterdam is home to numerous world-famous museums. No trip to the city is complete without visiting the Rijksmuseum, Van Gogh Museum, Anne Frank House or Stedelijk Museum.
